The dataset is extremely imbalanced, with fraud transactions representing only 0.17% of total data.
This means that 99.83% of the totality are legitimate transactions and not fraud. This leads to 
improper and inefficient predictions.
Therefore, accuracy alone is not an appropriate metric.
Metrics such as Precision, Recall, F1-score, and ROC-AUC will be more meaningful.

We calculated the average mean value of legit transactions and fraudulant transactions.
legit mean = 88.29 and fraud mean = 122.211
Now generally, fraud transactions are low to medium however here the fraud mean is bigger than the legit mean. 
But this can be because of a few fraud higher amout outliers. Hence, it is important to check the average median.

legit median = 22 and fraud median = 9.25
Now this tells us that the higher mean is only because of the few outliers. Otherwise, the fraud transactions are
ranged from low to medium only. 
//Hence, median is more robust than mean.

We use stratify=y in train/test split; why is it important?
If we randomly split without stratify:
We might accidentally put most fraud in train
and almost none in test.
That would destroy evaluation.
Stratify ensures:
Fraud percentage stays same in train and test.
